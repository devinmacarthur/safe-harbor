# Starseed's Core Argument: Debt Commitment = Real Ownership Stake

## The Sticking Point

Mae's parents provided 100% of the down payment.
But that doesn't mean they should own 100% of the property.

**Why?** Because Starseed took on 50% of the remaining debt -- and that debt is real liability, real risk, and real commitment.

---

## The Math of Who's "On the Hook"

### Total Purchase Price: $471,708

| Contribution Type | Mae's Side | Starseed's Side |
|-------------------|------------|-----------------|
| Down payment (cash) | $100,000 | $0 |
| Mortgage liability (50% each) | $188,683 | $188,683 |
| **Total commitment** | **$288,683** | **$188,683** |
| **% of total** | **60.5%** | **39.5%** |

Starseed is not a freeloader. Starseed signed the mortgage. Starseed is legally liable for $188,683 of debt.

---

## The Difference Between Cash and Commitment

### What Mae's Parents Risked
- $100,000 in cash
- If the house loses value, they could lose some or all of it
- Their money is "locked up" in the house

### What Starseed Risked
- **$188,683 in debt liability**
- If the house loses value, Starseed still owes the bank
- Starseed's credit, income, and financial future are on the line
- Starseed can't just "walk away" without consequences

**Key insight:** The down payment is "at risk" -- but so is Starseed's entire financial standing.

---

## Why 100% Down != 100% Ownership

The parents put down 100% of the *down payment*.
They did NOT put down 100% of the *purchase price*.

```
Purchase Price:     $471,708    (100%)
Down Payment:       $100,000    (21.2%)
Mortgage:           $377,366    (78.8%)
```

The parents funded 21% of the purchase.
Starseed is liable for 39.5% of the purchase (half the mortgage).
Mae is liable for the remaining 39.5% (half the mortgage).

**Why should 21% of the funding translate to 67-78% ownership** (as in Models 1 and 2)?

---

## What Starseed Is NOT Saying

Starseed is NOT saying:
- "The down payment doesn't matter"
- "Mae's parents shouldn't be protected"
- "I should own 50% from day one"

Starseed IS saying:
- "I took on real financial risk by co-signing the mortgage"
- "That risk should count as a contribution"
- "21% down payment shouldn't equal 70%+ ownership"

---

## The Emotional Core

Starseed's concern isn't about greed. It's about recognition.

> "I signed the same mortgage you did. I'm taking the same risk every month.
> I'm not asking for a free ride -- I'm asking for my commitment to count."

This is a reasonable position. The agreement should acknowledge it.

---

## Starseed's Position Summary

```
"I respect the $100k. I want your parents protected.

 But I also signed a $377k mortgage. I'm liable for half.
 That's not nothing. That's $188k of real commitment.

 I'm not asking for 50% from day one.
 I'm asking for my risk to count, not just your parents'."
```
