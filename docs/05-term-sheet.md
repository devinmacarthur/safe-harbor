## 1. OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE

The property is held as **Joint Tenants with Right of Survivorship** as recorded on the deed.

For purposes of equity distribution, the parties agree to a **Preferred Equity Waterfall** structure:

- **Layer 1 (Preferred Equity):** $100,000 contributed by Greg and Cynthia Lamb shall be returned first from any net sale proceeds.
- **Layer 2 (Improvement Credits):** Any documented capital improvements paid by one party shall be returned to that party.
- **Layer 3 (Equity Split):** Remaining proceeds shall be divided in proportion to each party's cumulative principal payments on the mortgage.

---

## 2. PAYMENT RESPONSIBILITIES

| Item | Mae's Share | Starseed's Share |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| Monthly mortgage payment | 50% ($1,545.05) | 50% ($1,545.05) |
| Property taxes (via escrow) | 50% | 50% |
| Homeowner's insurance | 50% | 50% |
| Utilities | 50% | 50% |
| Routine maintenance (<$500) | 50% | 50% |

**Payment tracking:** Both parties shall maintain records of all payments made. A shared spreadsheet or tracking document shall be updated monthly.

---

## 3. REPAIRS AND IMPROVEMENTS

### Routine Maintenance (under $500)
- Split 50/50
- Either party may authorize

### Major Repairs ($500 - $5,000)
- Split 50/50
- Requires mutual agreement before work begins
- Emergency repairs may proceed without consent if property is at risk

### Capital Improvements (over $5,000)
- Requires written mutual agreement
- Party paying more than 50% receives **Improvement Credit** equal to excess contribution
- Improvement Credits are repaid in Layer 2 of the sale waterfall

### Documentation Required
- Written estimate before work
- Receipts after completion
- Record of who paid what

## 4. EQUITY ACCOUNTING METHOD

### Monthly Tracking

```
Principal_Paid_This_Month = [from mortgage statement]
Mae_Principal_Share = Mae_Payment / Total_Payment
Starseed_Principal_Share = Starseed_Payment / Total_Payment

Running totals maintained:
  Mae_Cumulative_Principal
  Starseed_Cumulative_Principal
  Mae_Improvement_Credits
  Starseed_Improvement_Credits
```

### At Sale or Buyout

```
Net_Proceeds = Sale_Price - Selling_Costs - Mortgage_Payoff

Distribution Order:
  1. Return $100,000 Preferred Equity (to Mae/parents)
  2. Return Improvement Credits (to applicable party)
  3. Split remainder by Principal Contribution %

Mae_Share = Preferred + Mae_Improvements + (Remainder x Mae_Principal_%)
Starseed_Share = Starseed_Improvements + (Remainder x Starseed_Principal_%)
```

## 5. SALE WATERFALL FORMULA

### Complete Example (hypothetical $200,000 net proceeds)

```
Net Proceeds:                     $200,000

Step 1 - Preferred Equity:        -$100,000  -> Mae
Step 2 - Mae Improvements:        -$0        -> Mae
Step 2 - Starseed Improvements:   -$10,000   -> Starseed
Remaining:                        $90,000

Step 3 - Principal Split:
  Mae paid 50% of principal:      $45,000
  Starseed paid 50% of principal: $45,000

FINAL:
  Mae total:      $100,000 + $45,000 = $145,000
  Starseed total: $10,000 + $45,000 = $55,000
```

## 6. BUYOUT FORMULA

If one party wishes to buy out the other:

### Fair Market Value Determination
1. Average of two independent appraisals (each party selects one appraiser), OR
2. Mutual agreement on a single appraiser, OR
3. Recent comparable sales within 6 months

### Buyout Price Calculation

```
FMV = Fair Market Value
Mortgage_Balance = Current payoff amount
Hypothetical_Net = FMV x 0.94 - Mortgage_Balance  (assumes 6% selling costs)

Apply waterfall as if property were sold:
  Buying_Party_Owes = Other_Party's_Waterfall_Share
```

### Payment Terms
- Buyout must be completed within 90 days of agreement
- Buying party may finance the buyout
- Selling party must cooperate with deed transfer upon payment

---

## 7. DEFAULT RULES

### Missed Payment
- If one party fails to make their share of the monthly payment, the other party may cover it.
- The covering party receives **100% principal credit** for the covered amount.
- The non-paying party's principal share decreases accordingly.
- After 3 consecutive missed payments, the non-paying party is considered in default.

### Default Consequences
- Defaulting party must sell their interest to the non-defaulting party at calculated buyout price, OR
- Property shall be listed for sale within 60 days

### Grace Period
15 days from payment due date before coverage provisions apply.

---

## 8. RENTING POLICY

### Primary Residence Requirement
Both parties agree to occupy the property as primary residence.

### If One Party Moves Out
- Moving party continues to pay their 50% share of mortgage, taxes, insurance
- Remaining party does not owe rent to moving party
- Moving party may not rent their "share" to a third party without written consent

### Full Property Rental
- Requires written mutual consent
- Net rental income split 50/50
- Both parties remain responsible for mortgage regardless of tenant status

---

## 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### Step 1 -- Good Faith Discussion
Parties shall first attempt to resolve disputes through direct conversation.

### Step 2 -- Mediation
If unresolved after 30 days, parties shall engage a neutral mediator. Cost split 50/50.

### Step 3 -- Binding Arbitration
If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration under Oregon law. Cost split 50/50.

### No Litigation
Both parties waive the right to litigation except to enforce an arbitration award.

---

## 10. DEATH OR DISABILITY

### Death
- Due to Joint Tenancy with Right of Survivorship, the surviving owner automatically receives full ownership.
- Surviving owner assumes full mortgage responsibility.
- Surviving owner shall pay deceased party's estate according to the buyout formula within 180 days OR refinance to release estate from mortgage liability.

### Permanent Disability
- If one party becomes permanently unable to work, parties shall negotiate in good faith.
- Options include: buyout, sale, or modified payment arrangement.
- Neither party may force the other into foreclosure without first offering a buyout.

---

## 11. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

If either party wishes to sell their interest:
1. Selling party must provide written notice with proposed terms
2. Other party has 60 days to match terms and purchase
3. If declined, selling party may sell to third party at same or better terms
4. Any third-party buyer must agree to this co-ownership agreement

---

This agreement may only be amended by written consent of both parties.
