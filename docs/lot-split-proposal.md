# Lot Split Proposal: House + Land Trade

A proposal to resolve the Safe Harbor co-ownership by giving Mae the house and splitting the lot, with Starseed retaining an L-shaped parcel for Cascadia AI Systems Lab operations.

> **This is a discussion document, not legal advice.** Any lot partition requires city approval, surveying, and should involve a real estate attorney.

---

## The Concept

Instead of selling the property and splitting proceeds (where both parties lose money), resolve the co-ownership by dividing the asset:

- **Mae gets:** The house + front/primary lot parcel
- **Starseed gets:** An L-shaped parcel along the side and back of the property
- **Result:** Clean separation — no more shared mortgage, no more equity math, no forced sale

## Portland R7 Zoning: What's Allowed

Portland's R7 zone governs what lot sizes are possible in a partition:

| Requirement | R7 Standard |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Minimum lot size (standard)** | 4,200 sqft |
| **Minimum lot size (with ADU)** | 3,500 sqft |
| **Minimum lot depth** | 40 ft |
| **Flag lot pole width** | 12 ft minimum |
| **Flag lot minimum area** | Pole does NOT count toward lot area |
| **Flag lot setbacks** | Same on all sides (typically 5 ft in R7) |
| **Landscaping buffer** | 5 ft perimeter landscaping required for flag lots 3,000–10,000 sqft |

### What This Means for Us

With 10,890 sqft total, we can potentially create two lots if both meet the 4,200 sqft minimum (or 3,500 sqft under certain middle housing provisions). However, an L-shaped or flag lot configuration introduces the 12 ft access pole requirement, and the pole area doesn't count toward the minimum lot area.

**Feasibility range:**

| Mae's Parcel (House) | Starseed's Parcel (L-shape) | Notes |
|-----------------------|----------------------------|-------|
| 6,690 sqft | 4,200 sqft | Maximum land to Starseed (standard minimum) |
| 7,000 sqft | 3,890 sqft | R7 "standard" lot for Mae |
| 7,390 sqft | 3,500 sqft | Maximum land to Starseed (ADU/middle housing minimum) |
| 7,890 sqft | 3,000 sqft | Smaller Starseed parcel, generous house lot |

*Note: These are gross figures. The 12 ft access pole for the flag/L-shaped lot reduces usable area. A 12 ft × 60 ft pole = 720 sqft that doesn't count toward minimum lot area.*

## Financial Analysis: Why This Works

### The Current Problem ("The Impossible Math")

From the existing Safe Harbor analysis, if we sell in year 5:

```
Total everyone paid in:        $300,406
Total available at sale:        $158,394
Shortfall:                     -$142,012

You can only return 52.7 cents per dollar paid in.
```

Both parties lose significant money in a sale. The $142K went to interest, taxes, insurance, and selling costs — money that has already left the system.

### How the Lot Split Solves This

Instead of converting the property to cash (and losing 47.3% to transaction costs and sunk payments), both parties **keep a real asset**:

| | Sale Scenario (Year 5) | Lot Split Scenario |
|--|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Mae gets** | ~$131,631 cash | House + primary lot (sole ownership) |
| **Starseed gets** | ~$22,618 cash | L-shaped land parcel (sole ownership) |
| **Transaction costs** | ~$32,548 (6% selling costs) | Survey + partition costs (~$5,000–$15,000) |
| **Mae's mortgage situation** | Paid off from sale proceeds | Refinances into sole name (removes Starseed from loan) |

### Land Value Estimate

To determine fair value of the L-shaped parcel, we need to estimate land value per square foot. Methods to establish this:

1. **Tax assessment split** — Multnomah County assesses land and improvements separately. Check the property's assessed land value at [multcoproptax.com](https://multcoproptax.com/Property-Search).

2. **Comparable vacant lot sales** — Recent vacant lot sales in Gateway/Parkrose (97220) establish market rates. Portland metro residential land in similar neighborhoods typically ranges $15–$35/sqft.

3. **Residual land value** — Total property value minus improvement (house) value = land value.

**Estimated land values for Starseed's parcel:**

| Parcel Size | @ $15/sqft | @ $20/sqft | @ $25/sqft | @ $30/sqft |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 3,000 sqft | $45,000 | $60,000 | $75,000 | $90,000 |
| 3,500 sqft | $52,500 | $70,000 | $87,500 | $105,000 |
| 4,200 sqft | $63,000 | $84,000 | $105,000 | $126,000 |

### The Negotiation Trade

The core trade is straightforward:

**Starseed gives up:**
- 50% claim on the house
- Mortgage obligation (~$1,545/month)
- All rights to the house structure and improvements

**Starseed receives:**
- L-shaped land parcel (free and clear, no mortgage)
- Operational base for Cascadia AI Systems Lab
- Release from joint mortgage liability

**Mae gives up:**
- A strip of land along the side/back of the property
- Starseed as a co-owner (which they may view as a benefit)

**Mae receives:**
- 100% ownership of the house
- Sole control over the property (no more co-owner decisions)
- Ability to refinance into their name alone (potentially at a lower rate)
- Clean exit from the shared financial arrangement

### What Makes It Fair

Starseed has paid approximately $1,545/month × (number of months living there) into the property. In a sale scenario at year 5, Starseed would only receive ~$22,618 after Mae's priority return. A land parcel valued at $50,000–$100,000+ represents a **significantly better outcome** for Starseed than a sale — while also giving Mae exactly what they want: the house.

Mae keeps the house (the valuable asset) and eliminates the complexity of co-ownership. Their parents' $100,000 investment is protected in the house equity. They only give up land they may not be using.

## How the Lot Split Would Work

### L-Shape Configuration

```
              STREET (NE 106th Ave)
    ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                 │
    │         MAE'S LOT               │
    │        (House +                 │
    │         front yard)             │
    │                                 │
    │              ┌──────────────────┤
    │              │                  │
    │              │   STARSEED'S     │
    │              │   LOT            │
    ├──────────────┤   (L-shape:      │
    │              │    side +        │
    │  STARSEED'S  │    back)         │
    │  LOT (cont.) │                  │
    │              │                  │
    └──────────────┴──────────────────┘
                  BACK OF LOT
```

*Actual dimensions and shape require a professional survey and must meet Portland R7 standards.*

The L-shape provides:
- Street access or access easement (12 ft minimum for flag lot pole)
- Enough width for vehicle parking/staging
- Separation from the house living areas
- Back-of-lot space for equipment/structures

### Partition Process (Portland)

1. **Pre-application conference** — Meet with Bureau of Development Services ($300–$500)
2. **Hire surveyor** — Professional land survey ($2,000–$5,000)
3. **Submit preliminary plan** — Land use review application with maps
4. **City review** — Planners check zoning compliance; other departments check utilities, stormwater, streets
5. **Approval** — Conditions may include utility easements, access requirements
6. **Final plat** — Surveyed final layout filed with county
7. **Recording** — New parcels recorded with Multnomah County

**Timeline:** 6–9 months typical
**Estimated cost:** $5,000–$15,000 total (survey, fees, filing)

### Mortgage Considerations

The current mortgage is in both names. For the lot split to work:

- **Mae refinances** the mortgage into their name only, secured by their parcel (house + lot)
- **Starseed is released** from the joint mortgage obligation
- **Lender approval** is needed — Guild Mortgage must approve the partition and release Starseed from the note
- **Mae must qualify** for the refinance on their own (or with a co-signer)

This is a critical step. If Mae cannot refinance on their own, alternatives include:
- Mae's parents co-sign the refinance
- Starseed remains on the mortgage for a transition period with a written agreement
- Sell the property instead (fallback)

## Scenarios to Discuss

### Scenario A: Equal Value Trade
- Starseed's land parcel value ≈ Starseed's equity claim (~$22,618 at year 5)
- Smaller parcel (~1,500–2,500 sqft at $15/sqft)
- **Pro:** Strictly "fair" by the numbers
- **Con:** May be too small for meaningful business use; may not meet R7 minimums

### Scenario B: Starseed Gets More Land, Compensates Difference
- Larger parcel (3,500–4,200 sqft)
- Starseed pays Mae the difference between land value and equity claim
- Example: 4,200 sqft × $20/sqft = $84,000 value; Starseed's equity claim = $22,618; Starseed pays Mae $61,382
- **Pro:** Both parties get what they need; mathematically clean
- **Con:** Starseed needs cash or financing for the payment

### Scenario C: Sweat Equity / Future Value Trade
- Larger parcel to Starseed
- Value difference offset by Starseed's past/future contributions:
  - Monthly mortgage payments already made
  - Improvements to property
  - Agreement to maintain shared fence/landscaping
  - Below-market services from the drone lab (aerial property photos, etc.)
- **Pro:** Creative, avoids cash outlay
- **Con:** Harder to quantify; requires trust

### Scenario D: Starseed Buys at Discount
- Mae agrees to sell the parcel at a friend/partner discount
- Reflects that Starseed has been paying half the mortgage on the entire property
- Example: $84,000 market value sold at $40,000–$50,000
- **Pro:** Simple cash transaction; Starseed gets clear title
- **Con:** Mae may feel they're giving up value

## Key Questions to Resolve

1. **What is the actual land value?** — Pull the Multnomah County assessment and/or get comparable vacant lot sales in the area
2. **What parcel size works for both?** — Mae needs enough yard; Starseed needs enough for the mobile lab
3. **Can Mae refinance alone?** — This is the biggest practical hurdle
4. **Does the L-shape meet R7 partition requirements?** — Need a pre-application conference with Portland PPD
5. **What about the access pole?** — 12 ft wide access strip to the street is required; where does it go?
6. **Utilities?** — Does the Starseed parcel need separate utility connections (water, sewer, electric)?
7. **What can be built on the parcel?** — R7 allows accessory structures; confirm with the city what's permitted for business use
8. **Timeline?** — When does this happen relative to the current living arrangement?

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Pull the tax assessment** — Visit [multcoproptax.com](https://multcoproptax.com/Property-Search) and get the land vs. improvement value breakdown
2. **Get comparable sales** — Ask a realtor for recent vacant lot sales in Gateway/Parkrose 97220
3. **Schedule a pre-application conference** — Portland Bureau of Development Services, (503) 823-7300. Cost ~$300–$500. They'll tell you exactly what's feasible on your specific lot.
4. **Talk to Mae** — Present the concept and gauge their interest before spending money on surveys/applications
5. **Talk to Guild Mortgage** — Ask about the process for releasing one borrower after a lot partition
6. **Consult a CPA** — Understand the tax implications for both parties (capital gains, business deductions, etc.)
7. **Consult a real estate attorney** — Any partition agreement needs legal review
