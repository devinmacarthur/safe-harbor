# Recommendation Summary

## One-Page Overview for Starseed

---

## The Situation

- You and Mae bought a house together for $471,708
- Mae's parents provided $100,000 down payment (21%)
- You both pay 50% of the $3,090/month mortgage
- You both live there and are on the deed as Joint Tenants
- Expected hold: 5-7 years
- **Problem:** No written agreement on how to split equity at sale

---

## The Core Insight: Debt Commitment Counts

| Party | Cash | Debt Liability | Total Commitment | % |
|-------|------|----------------|------------------|---|
| Mae (+ parents) | $100,000 | $188,683 | $288,683 | **60.5%** |
| Starseed | $0 | $188,683 | $188,683 | **39.5%** |

**You signed the same mortgage Mae did.** That's $188,683 of personal liability -- real risk that should count as a contribution from day one.

---

## Six Models Considered

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **1. Total Cash** | Ownership = all cash contributed | Mae (permanently) |
| **2. Debt First** | 50/50 ownership, but $100k repaid first | Mae |
| **3. Waterfall** | $100k first, then split by principal paid | Mae |
| **4. Earn-In** | Start unequal, shift to 50/50 over time | Starseed |
| **5. Commitment** | Split by total commitment (60.5/39.5) | Starseed |
| **6. Commit+Floor** | Commitment split, but $100k protected | **Both** |

---

## Recommendation: Model 6 -- Commitment with Floor Protection

### How It Works

```
In GOOD markets (Mae's share > $100k):
  -> Split by commitment: Mae 60.5% / Starseed 39.5%

In BAD markets (Mae's share < $100k):
  -> Floor protection: Mae gets $100k first, Starseed gets remainder
```

### Why This Is Best

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Recognizes your debt** | Your $188k mortgage liability counts as contribution |
| **Protects parents** | In bad markets, they still get $100k first |
| **Fair in good markets** | You get 39.5%, not just scraps after $100k |
| **Natural compromise** | Middle ground on the negotiation spectrum |
| **Minimizes resentment** | Both parties' contributions are recognized |

## The Trade-Off You're Offering

In exchange for Model 6, you accept:

- **In flat markets:** You get $0, parents get everything available
- **In down markets:** You get $0, parents get priority
- **Floor protection:** Mae never gets less than $100k if equity exists

This shows good faith -- you're not asking for upside without accepting downside protection for Mae's parents.
