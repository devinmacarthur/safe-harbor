# Email Drafts for Mae

Two versions of the initial email to open the conversation.

---

## Version 1: Simple and Direct

**Subject:** Thinking about our house stuff -- can we chat soon?

---

Hey Mae,

I've been thinking about something and wanted to share it with you before we talk in person.

When we bought the house, we didn't really nail down what happens when we eventually sell -- like how we'd split things up. Your parents put in the $100K down payment (which was huge and made this possible), and we've been splitting the mortgage 50/50 since then. But we never actually wrote down how ownership works.

I want to make sure we figure this out now, while we're on good terms, rather than later when it might be more stressful.

### The Core Question

How do we fairly account for:
- Your parents' $100K down payment
- The mortgage payments we're both making every month

### What I've Been Thinking About

Your parents' $100K should absolutely be protected -- that was their money and they took a risk on us. I'm 100% on board with making sure they get that back (and ideally more if the house appreciates).

At the same time, I'm on the hook for half of a $377K mortgage. That's roughly $189K I've committed to pay over time. I want to make sure that commitment counts for something too.

### A Possible Approach

1. **Recognize everyone's commitment** -- your parents' $100K plus both of our mortgage obligations
2. **Protect your parents first** -- if the market goes bad, they get their $100K back before we split anything
3. **Share appreciation fairly** -- if the house goes up in value, we both benefit proportionally

This isn't about me trying to get more than I deserve. It's about making sure we both feel like the arrangement is fair.

Can we sit down soon and talk through some options? I want to hear how you see it too.

-- Starseed

## Version 2: Game Theory Framing

**Subject:** Can we talk about our house agreement soon?

---

Hey Mae,

I've been doing a lot of thinking about our house situation -- specifically what happens down the road when we sell, and how we'd split things up. We never really nailed that down, and I think it's worth figuring out now while we're on the same page.

### The Big Picture

Your parents' $100K made this possible. Full stop. That money should be protected, and they should get it back (and more, if the house appreciates). No argument from me there.

At the same time, I'm carrying half of a $377K mortgage -- that's a real commitment I've made, and I want to make sure that counts for something too.

### Two Ways to Think About It

**Approach 1: Partners**
We treat each other as equals. Your parents get their $100K back first, then we split everything else 50/50. Simple. No scorekeeping. We're in this together.

**Approach 2: Investors**
Ownership is based on who's invested more capital. Whoever puts in more money owns a bigger share of the profits.

I want to be honest with you -- I'm genuinely happy with either approach. But they lead to pretty different outcomes, and I think we should decide together which one feels right for *us*.

### What I'd Like

Can we sit down this week and talk through this? I've done some research and have a few scenarios we can look at together. But mostly I just want to hear what you're thinking, and figure out something we both feel good about.

This isn't about "winning" for me. It's about making sure we're both on the same team.

Let me know when works for you?

-- Starseed

---

### Notes on the Emails

**Version 1** is best if you want to keep it simple and non-confrontational.

**Version 2** subtly introduces the cooperative vs. competitive frame. It:
- Makes clear you're happy with either approach
- Positions "Partners" as the simpler option
- Doesn't threaten, but signals you've thought this through
- Leaves room for the competitive counter-move if needed later
