# Six Ownership Models Compared

## Baseline Numbers

```
Purchase Price:           $471,708
Down Payment:             $100,000  (21.2%)
Mortgage:                 $377,366  (78.8%)
Monthly P&I:              $2,670.97
  - Principal portion:    ~$285/month (grows slowly over time)
  - Interest portion:     ~$2,386/month (decreases over time)

At 50/50 split:
  - Each pays:            $1,545.05/month total
  - Each pays principal:  ~$142.50/month
```

---

## MODEL 1 -- Total Cash Ownership

### Concept
Ownership is proportional to total lifetime cash contributed (down payment + all mortgage payments).

### Who It Benefits
Mae initially (because $100k is a huge head start), but Starseed catches up slowly over time.

### Emotional Risk Level
**HIGH** for Starseed --- feels like permanent minority owner.

### Math Example (Year 5)

```
Mae's contributions:
  Down payment (parents):     $100,000
  5 years of payments:        $1,545.05 x 60 = $92,703
  TOTAL:                      $192,703

Starseed's contributions:
  5 years of payments:        $1,545.05 x 60 = $92,703
  TOTAL:                      $92,703

Combined contributions:       $285,406

Mae's ownership:      $192,703 / $285,406 = 67.5%
Starseed's ownership: $92,703 / $285,406 = 32.5%
```

### Ownership Trajectory

| Year | Mae's Total | Starseed's Total | Mae % | Starseed % |
|------|-------------|------------------|-------|------------|
| 0 | $100,000 | $0 | 100% | 0% |
| 2 | $137,081 | $37,081 | 78.7% | 21.3% |
| 5 | $192,703 | $92,703 | 67.5% | 32.5% |
| 7 | $229,784 | $129,784 | 63.9% | 36.1% |
| 10 | $285,406 | $185,406 | 60.6% | 39.4% |
| 30 | $656,418 | $556,418 | 54.1% | 45.9% |

### Verdict
Starseed never reaches 50% even after 30 years. This model treats the down payment as permanent advantage. **High resentment risk for Starseed.**

---

## MODEL 2 -- Down Payment as Separate Debt

### Concept
Ownership is 50/50 from day one, but the $100k down payment is treated as a debt owed to Mae's parents that must be repaid first at sale.

### Who It Benefits
Both, in different ways. Starseed gets equal ownership. Parents get their money back.

### Emotional Risk Level
**LOW** --- feels fair to both parties.

### Math Example (Sale at Year 5 with 20% appreciation)

```
Sale price:                   $471,708 x 1.20 = $566,050
Selling costs (6%):           $33,963
Remaining mortgage (~Y5):     ~$356,000
Net proceeds:                 $176,087

Distribution:
  Step 1: Repay parents       $100,000
  Remaining:                  $76,087
  Step 2: Split 50/50
    Mae:                      $38,044
    Starseed:                 $38,044

TOTAL PAYOUTS:
  Mae (+ parents):            $138,044
  Starseed:                   $38,044
```

### Verdict
Simple, clean, easy to explain. Parents protected. Starseed isn't permanently disadvantaged. **Moderate resentment risk** --- Mae might feel Starseed gets "free" appreciation.

---

## MODEL 3 -- Preferred Equity Waterfall (RECOMMENDED)

### Concept
The $100k down payment is treated as "preferred equity" that gets returned first, THEN remaining equity is split based on principal payments made by each party.

### Who It Benefits
Both. Parents protected. Starseed builds real ownership through principal payments.

### Emotional Risk Level
**LOWEST** --- acknowledges both the upfront risk AND ongoing contribution.

### Why This Is Sophisticated
It separates "return OF capital" (getting your money back) from "return ON capital" (appreciation/equity growth). This is how professional investors think.

### Math Example (Sale at Year 5 with 20% appreciation)

```
Sale price:                   $566,050
Selling costs (6%):           $33,963
Remaining mortgage:           ~$356,000
Net proceeds:                 $176,087

WATERFALL DISTRIBUTION:

Layer 1 -- Return Preferred Equity (Parents' $100k)
  Paid to Mae/parents:        $100,000
  Remaining:                  $76,087

Layer 2 -- Split by Principal Paid
  Total principal paid (5 yrs): ~$21,366
  Mae's share (50%):          $10,683  ->  50% of $76,087 = $38,044
  Starseed's share (50%):     $10,683  ->  50% of $76,087 = $38,044

TOTAL PAYOUTS:
  Mae (+ parents):            $138,044
  Starseed:                   $38,044
```

### Verdict
Most flexible and fair. Rewards actual principal contributions. Protects parents' capital. **Lowest resentment risk.**

## MODEL 4 -- Earn-In Equity Model

### Concept
Start with ownership split based on initial contributions, then ownership shifts toward 50/50 over time as payments are made.

### Who It Benefits
Mae early, Starseed late. Creates a "vesting" dynamic.

### Emotional Risk Level
**MEDIUM** --- feels like Starseed is "earning" his way in, which can feel demeaning.

### Earn-In Schedule

| Year | Mae % | Starseed % | Trigger |
|------|-------|------------|---------|
| 0 | 60.5% | 39.5% | Starting position |
| 2 | 58% | 42% | 24 on-time payments |
| 5 | 54% | 46% | 60 on-time payments |
| 7 | 52% | 48% | 84 on-time payments |
| 10 | 50% | 50% | 120 on-time payments |

### Verdict
Can feel patronizing to Starseed ("earning" what should be equal ownership). Also, doesn't protect the $100k specifically. **Medium resentment risk.**

---

## MODEL 5 -- Commitment-Based Ownership

### Concept
Ownership equals total commitment (cash + debt liability) from day one. Recognizes that signing the mortgage is real "skin in the game."

### The Key Insight
Starseed signed the mortgage --- that's $188,683 of personal liability. This is real risk that should count as a contribution.

### Commitment Breakdown

| Party | Cash | Debt Liability | Total Commitment | Ownership |
|-------|------|----------------|------------------|-----------|
| Mae | $100,000 | $188,683 | $288,683 | **60.5%** |
| Starseed | $0 | $188,683 | $188,683 | **39.5%** |

### Math Example (Sale at Year 5 with 20% appreciation)

```
Sale price:                   $566,050
Selling costs (6%):           $33,963
Remaining mortgage:           ~$356,000
Net proceeds:                 $176,087

Distribution by commitment:
  Mae (60.5%):                $106,533
  Starseed (39.5%):           $69,554
```

### Comparison to Model 3

| Scenario | Model 3 Mae | Model 3 Starseed | Model 5 Mae | Model 5 Starseed |
|----------|-------------|------------------|-------------|------------------|
| Year 5, +20% | $138,044 | $38,044 | $106,533 | $69,554 |
| Year 5, Flat | $87,406 | $0 | $52,881 | $34,525 |
| Year 5, -10% | $43,065 | $0 | $26,054 | $17,011 |

### Verdict
Recognizes Starseed's debt commitment as real contribution. More equitable in growth scenarios. However, **doesn't specifically protect parents' $100k** --- in flat/down markets, they may not recover their full investment.

---

## MODEL 6 -- Commitment + Floor Protection (COMPROMISE)

### Concept
Split by commitment ratio (60.5/39.5), BUT Mae's payout can never be less than $100,000 if sufficient equity exists. Best of both worlds.

### Who It Benefits
Both. Starseed's debt commitment is recognized. Mae's parents have downside protection.

### Emotional Risk Level
**LOWEST** --- addresses both parties' core concerns.

### How It Works

```
Step 1: Calculate commitment-based split
  Mae_Base = Net_Proceeds x 60.5%
  Starseed_Base = Net_Proceeds x 39.5%

Step 2: Apply floor protection
  IF Mae_Base >= $100,000:
    Use commitment split (60.5/39.5)
  ELSE IF Net_Proceeds >= $100,000:
    Mae gets $100,000, Starseed gets remainder
  ELSE:
    Mae gets all (insufficient to cover $100k)
```

### Math Examples

**Year 5, 20% Appreciation ($176,087 net):**
```
Mae_Base = $176,087 x 60.5% = $106,533  (> $100k)
Starseed_Base = $176,087 x 39.5% = $69,554

RESULT: Mae $106,533, Starseed $69,554
(Same as Model 5 -- floor not triggered)
```

**Year 5, Flat Market ($87,406 net):**
```
Mae_Base = $87,406 x 60.5% = $52,881  (< $100k!)
Net_Proceeds < $100,000

RESULT: Mae $87,406, Starseed $0
(Floor triggered -- parents protected)
```

### When Does the Floor Trigger?

| Net Proceeds | Mae at 60.5% | Floor Triggered? | Mae Gets | Starseed Gets |
|--------------|--------------|------------------|----------|---------------|
| $80,000 | $48,400 | Yes | $80,000 | $0 |
| $120,000 | $72,600 | Yes | $100,000 | $20,000 |
| $165,289 | $100,000 | Threshold | $100,000 | $65,289 |
| $176,087 | $106,533 | No | $106,533 | $69,554 |
| $220,000 | $133,100 | No | $133,100 | $86,900 |

### Verdict
**The compromise model.** Recognizes Starseed's debt commitment in good markets. Protects Mae's parents in weak markets. Both parties can feel this is fair. **Recommended for negotiation.**

## Recommendation

### For Maximum Parent Protection: Model 3 (Preferred Equity Waterfall)
- Parents' $100k returned first in all scenarios
- Starseed only gets equity after parents are made whole

### For Balanced Fairness: Model 6 (Commitment + Floor Protection)
- Recognizes Starseed's debt commitment as real contribution
- Protects parents' $100k as a floor in weak markets
- In strong markets, Starseed gets meaningful equity ($70k vs $38k at Year 5)
- **Recommended as the compromise position**

### For Maximum Debt Recognition: Model 5 (Pure Commitment)
- Starseed's mortgage liability counts from day one
- 60.5/39.5 split reflects total commitment
- Parents not specifically protected
