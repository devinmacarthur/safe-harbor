# Joint Venture Spreadsheet Analysis

Detailed spreadsheets analyzing the real estate joint venture from every angle.

---

## Overview

These spreadsheets compare different ownership models, rental scenarios, appreciation rates, and capital improvements for the property.

**Property Details:**
- Purchase Price: $459,000 (initial analysis)
- Down Payment: $87,210 (Partner B only)
- Mortgage: $371,790 at 7.625% interest
- Monthly Payment: $3,085.26
- Planned Hold: 5 years (with flexibility to extend)

---

## The 3 Models Compared

### Model A: Tiered Equity
- Partner B owns 100% of down payment equity
- Monthly principal split 50/50
- After 5 years: Partner A ~9%, Partner B ~91%
- **Not recommended** due to capital improvement disincentive

### Model B: 50/50 + Priority Return
- Equal ownership from day one
- Partner B gets first $111,297 at sale
- Remaining split 50/50
- **Good option** if no solo improvements planned

### Model C: 50/50 + Priority Return + Improvement Credits (Recommended)
- Same as Model B, plus 75% equity credit for solo improvements
- Solves the improvement incentive problem
- **Recommended** for the 5-year hold

---

## Key Findings

### Without Rental Income (5-year hold, 3% appreciation)
- Partner A loses: **-$71,082** (-76.8% ROI)
- Partner B loses: **-$46,995** (-26.1% ROI)
- **Both partners lose significant money!**

### With Rental Income
- Partner A loss reduced by **91%** (from -$71k to -$6k)
- **Rental income is ESSENTIAL for this to work financially**

### Break-Even Analysis
- Without rental: need 6-8% annual appreciation (very high)
- With rental: ~7 year hold at 3% appreciation (reasonable)
- Historical US average: 3-4% annually

## Waterfall vs Preferred/Common Equity

Both achieve **identical financial outcomes** but frame it differently:

### Waterfall Distribution (Recommended)
"Partner B, you own the initial equity. When we sell, proceeds distribute in order: First, you get your $87,210 back. Second, you get 5% annual return. Third, improvement credits. Fourth, we split remaining profits 50/50."

### Preferred/Common Equity
"Your $87,210 is Preferred Equity (100% yours, gets 5% return). Everything else is Common Equity (owned 50/50). At sale, Preferred liquidates first, then Common distributes."

### Financial Comparison (Year 5 Sale)

| Metric | Waterfall | Preferred/Common | Difference |
|--------|-----------|------------------|------------|
| Partner A Receives | $22,618 | $22,618 | $0 |
| Partner B Receives | $131,631 | $131,631 | $0 |

**They're financially IDENTICAL!** Choose Waterfall for simplicity.

## Recommended Structure

**Ownership Model: Model C (50/50 + Priority Return + Improvement Credits)**

| Term | Value |
|------|-------|
| Ownership | 50/50 from day one |
| Priority return | $87,210 x (1.05)^5 = $111,297 |
| Improvement credit | 75% equity credit to paying partner |
| Remaining split | 50/50 after priority + improvements |
| Rental income | 50/50 after $500/month preference to Partner B |
| Timeline | 5 years minimum, flexible extension |
| Exit | Either partner can trigger at year 5, other has first right to buy |
